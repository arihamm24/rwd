Addition

Addition is the manner by which we combine numbers in mathematics. It is represented by the plus sign (+).

  • To add, you take one number and decide how many places down a number line you will move it forward
  • 2 + 2 = 2 + 1 + 1 = 3 + 1 = 4

Subtraction

Subtraction is the manner by which we decrease numbers in mathematics. It is represented by the minus sign (-).

  • To subtract, you take one number and decide how many places down a number line you will move it backward
  • 5 - 2 = 5 - 1 - 1 = 4 - 1 = 3

Multiplication

Multiplication is the manner by which we compound numbers in mathematics. It is most commonly represented by an irregular sign (in programming we use "*" ).

  • Multiplication is repeated addition! When you multiply a number by a factor, you simply add that number to itself however many times the factor is.
  • 3 * 3 = 3 + 3 + 3 = 6 + 3 = 9

Division

Division is the manner by which we combine numbers in mathematics. It is frequently represented by a fraction bar (/).

  • Division is repeated subtraction! When you divide a number by a factor, you simply subtract that factor from the original number until you get 0. However many times you subtracted is your answer
  • 6/2= 6 - 2 = 4 - 2 = 2 - 2 = 0
  • 6/2 = 3

Advanced Concepts

These concepts build on the basic arithmetic operations, but they may take some time to master.

  1. Remainders (Division)
    • Sometimes when we divide, the quotients will not be round numbers and we will have leftover values. Those values are called remainders
    • 45/8 = 5 r5
      • 8 * 5 = 40, but we're still 5 shy of 45. That makes 5 a remainder!
  2. Exponents (Multiplication)
    • Exponents are repeated multiplication. When a number is being operated with an exponent, it is being "raised to a power"
    • 2 ^ 3 = 2 * 2 * 2 = 4 * 2 = 8
  3. Negative Numbers (Subtraction)
    • When you are first taught subtraction, you are told numbers cannot go below 0, meaning you cannot subtract a larger number from anoter smaller number (i.e. 3 - 5 cannot be solved). In higher math, you learn about negative numbers, which throw this rule out the window.
    • 3 - 5 = 3 - 3 - 2 = 0 - 2 = -2
